Why I built this.

Like every junior, I started my internship search in Google Sheets. It worked for the first 20 cold emails, but broke down completely by the 100th. Trying to track which engineers I emailed, who replied, and who I needed to follow up with became a nightmare of endless tabs and copy-pasted templates.

Existing CRMs were designed for sales teams with enterprise quotas, not students targeting SWE roles. I didn't need to track "$50k ARR pipelines". I just needed a fast, offline system to give me deep context before I hit send.

So I engineered it myself. No monthly subscriptions, no sluggish web apps. Just a blazing fast local tool to get the offer.

Decisions I made while building this.

Architecture

Local-first, always.

No server means no recurring cost, no data leaving your machine, and no dependency on my uptime. The tradeoff: no sync across devices in v0.1. Worth it.

Scope

Deferred the AI layer.

I had planned AI-assisted email drafting for v0.1. I cut it deliberately to ship faster. A product that ships without AI beats a product that never ships with it.

Pricing

Free, not freemium.

There's no $9 tier. This is a personal project I'm sharing publicly. If it grows into something more, I'll revisit — but not before doing the market research to justify it.

Privacy First

Your data.
Your machine.
Full stop.

No cloud sync. No telemetry. No vendor lock-in. The entire database is encrypted with SQLCipher AES-256. Your master key lives in the OS Keychain — not our servers.

SQLCipher AES-256 OS Keychain integration AES-256-GCM field encryption Zero telemetry Offline-first

Pricing

Free. No catch.

This is a personal project. I built it for myself, open-sourced it, and you can use it for free. There's no paid tier, no subscription, no server sending your data anywhere. It runs on your machine.
